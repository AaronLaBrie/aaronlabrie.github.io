Aaron's Miso - アーロンの味噌

I more or less just followed the "It's Alive" take on this, let it ride for a year, and it turned out great! If you wanna make your own or find yummy stuff to use it in, check your local Asian grocery. I like Asahi up on Burnet.

This is a one-year-old 'red' miso. Same ingredients as the 'white' miso you might have seen before, you just let it ferment longer. It doesn't really need to be refrigerated but you can if you'd like. It's technically still fermenting which is neat! If anything icky grows on the top just scrape it off. If it gives off any liquid that's just tamari (gluten-free soy sauce!) and you can either mix it back up or use it separately. If you prefer a finer texture you can just toss it in a food processor, totally your call.

I've done some recipes (and stolen the website colors from) Bonnie Chung's great cookbook "Miso Tasty" and can safely recommend it. Here's her recipe for a very basic miso soup, just like what you get to start your meal at any Japanese restaurant. She likes to use both red and white miso, so if you'd like to follow the recipe exactly go pick up some white miso.
