Aaron's Miso - アーロンの味噌

I more or less just followed the It's Alive take on this, let it ride for a year, and it turned out tasty! If you wanna make your own or find yummy stuff to use it in, check your local Asian grocery, such as Asahi up on Burnet. Also check out the great cookbook Miso Tasty by Bonnie Chung.

This is a one-year-old 'red' miso. Same ingredients as the 'white' miso you might have seen before, you just let it ferment longer. It's technically still fermenting which is neat! It doesn't really need to be refrigerated but you can if you'd like. If anything icky grows on the top just scrape it off. If it gives off any liquid that's just tamari (gluten-free soy sauce!) and you can either mix it back up or use it separately.

Here's a classic miso soup recipe to get started. The dashi can be store-bought, and you can make/buy it vegetarian as well. You may want to toss some miso into a food processor before making this - I think that's better for making soups or anything that you want it to fully dissolve in.

Have fun!
